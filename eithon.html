Eithon - The Hackathon

In the recent past, Kerala went through one of its most unfavorable periods of all times. It encountered two fatal floods consecutively in the year 2018 and 2019. In 2018, an unusually heavy monsoon rainfall (about 116% more than usual rainfall) in the state resulted in dams filling up to maximum capacities, leading 35 of its 54 dams to be opened. A sudden release of water simultaneously from different reservoirs during extreme rainfall aggravated the damage.

Heavy rains have caused severe landslides in hilly districts, leaving them isolated. All 14 districts of the state were placed on red alert. Over 483 lives were lost and 140 were reported missing. The flooding has affected hundreds of villages, destroyed an estimated 10,000 km of roads and thousands of homes have been damaged or destroyed. About 1,247,496 people have found shelter in over 3,274 relief camps. Soon after the flood, the state was caught under the threat of a severe drought. Water level in wells, ponds and rivers have recorded lowest levels and some wells even collapsed.

Owing to the physical and population density of cities, environmental threats like flooding results in both devastating financial loss and deaths. How can we make our cities resilient against this?
